Riding Changes Everything

For most kids, hopping on a bike is second nature. But for children with cerebral palsy, spina bifida, muscular dystrophy, and other conditions, standard bikes simply don't work.

Adaptive bikes can cost between $1,000 and $7,000, far out of reach for many families. Insurance rarely covers them. That's where we come in.

Pedals of Hope bridges the gap between what families need and what they can afford, so no child is left watching from the sidewalk.

How It Works

Three simple steps to get your child riding

1. Apply

Fill out our simple application with your child's needs and a letter from their therapist or doctor. We review every application personally.

2. Get Matched

Our team works with certified adaptive equipment specialists to find the perfect bike or trike for your child's specific abilities and goals.

3. Ride!

Your child receives their custom-fitted adaptive bike, fully funded or with significant financial assistance. Then the real fun begins.

Equipment We Fund

Specialized adaptive equipment tailored to each child

Adaptive Tricycles

Hand-and-foot powered trikes with full trunk and lateral support for children who need extra stability.

Hand Cycles

Upper-body powered cycles for children with limited or no leg function. Recumbent and upright options available.

Power-Assist Add-Ons

Electric assist attachments that help children with limited strength enjoy longer rides without exhaustion.

Custom Modifications

Specialized seating, harnesses, pedal adaptations, and steering modifications fitted to each child's needs.
