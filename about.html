About Pedals of Hope

Our mission, our story, and the people behind the wheels

Our Mission

Pedals of Hope exists to ensure that every child, regardless of physical ability or financial circumstance, has access to the adaptive cycling equipment they need to ride a bike.

We believe bike riding is more than recreation. It builds confidence, strengthens muscles, improves coordination, and most importantly, lets kids be kids alongside their friends and siblings.

Since our founding, we've provided over 250 adaptive bikes to children in 18 states, partnering with therapists, equipment manufacturers, and generous donors to make it happen.

Our Story

Pedals of Hope started in 2019 when our founder, Jane Doe, watched her daughter's best friend sit on the sidelines during a neighborhood bike ride. The friend had cerebral palsy and needed an adaptive trike that cost over $4,000. Insurance wouldn't cover it.

Jane organized a community fundraiser, raised the money in three weeks, and watched that little girl ride for the first time. The look on her face changed everything.

"One bike turned into five. Five turned into fifty. We couldn't stop, because the need never stops." Today, Pedals of Hope operates nationwide, processing applications year-round.

What We Believe

The values that guide everything we do

Inclusion First

Every child deserves to participate. Disability should never mean exclusion from the simple joys of childhood.

Dignity Always

We treat every family with respect. Our process is simple, private, and judgment-free. No family should feel embarrassed asking for help.

Right Fit, Every Time

We don't do one-size-fits-all. Every child is matched with equipment specifically suited to their abilities, goals, and body.

Transparency

92 cents of every dollar goes directly to equipment and fitting. We publish our financials annually and keep overhead minimal.
